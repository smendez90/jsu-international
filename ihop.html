Graduate Programs

If you have just completed a Bachelor’s degree program, or you are looking to advance in your career with higher degree, we offer close to 30 Graduate programs at Master’s, Post-Graduate specialist and PhD levels. Having an advanced degree will/may give you an opportunity to negotiate a better career option and higher financial reward. As per IIE open door report, close to 383,000 international students chose to study in the United States for their graduate degree.

There are thousands of Institutions offering graduate programs within the United States. Thus, it is important that International students consider the Accreditation of the Graduate programs that they are looking for and Cost of attendance when selecting the University to pursue their graduate program.

Here at JSU, all of our academic programs are well accredited. Some of our programs are accredited by worldwide accreditation bodies such as:

Our Computer Science and Information System is recognized by Computing Accreditation commission of ABET Our school of business and industry is recognized by AACSB International

For list of Accreditation, click here.

We at Jacksonville State University recognize the need for our students to be global citizens to compete in the real world once they graduate. We therefore celebrate diversity and welcome students from all around the world.

After Graduation

International Students under F-1 visa category can apply to work Under OPT (Optional Practical training) in the United States for 12 months after graduating from their degree program. Students in STEM field are eligible for 24 extra months to be employed in their field of study. JSU international alumni are employed by Fortune 100 companies.

International Students may want to consider pursuing a Doctoral degree program depending on their career goal.

Transfer

Did you just start graduate degree program at another institution and you are now wanting to continue the program here at JSU? Let us help you transition to JSU easily.

The number of semester hours of graduate credit that may be transferred from a regionally accredited institution to JSU toward a graduate degree program is limited to the following maximums:

6 hours toward a 30 semester hour program;

9 hours toward a 33 semester hour program; and

12 hours toward a program of 36 or more semester hours.

Evaluation of transfer credit is made by the appropriate department of the student’s major and approved by the Dean/Director of Graduate Studies. Evaluation must be done at the time of admission before enrollment.

Transfer credit cannot be used to raise the GPA of JSU course work to the required minimum GPA of specified majors, teacher certification programs, or graduate certificate programs.
